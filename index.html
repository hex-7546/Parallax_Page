Autumn Flowers

Autumn starts in August, rather than the September Equinox. It’s September now, but the temperatures still hover in the 80s, thunderstorms still threaten my daily sushi pickup, and cicadas still squawk through the trees like tortured squirrels. Another common motif in this season’s haiku is the wind, sometimes called “the voice of autumn,” the sound of crunchy leaves rustling in the distance, or “autumn music,” the sound of rain falling on those leaves. “Autumn wind” seems to be the favored choice for translators, though, which can make for some monotonous haiku.
